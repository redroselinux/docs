# Why another distro?

So you probably came here thinking that this is another Arch-based distribution that does nothing anyway, just NO. This distribution is independent. Very hard to believe in the world of new distros, right?

## Okay, but does it have purpose?

Well, yeah. I mean, for me at least. There is a lot of things I do not like about some distros. And I have decided to combine many features of other distros into one distro with my own opinions.

## History of the project

When I was 8 years old, I seriously, seriously wanted to make an operating system. In fact, this is what led me to start coding. I used the Turtle library in Python to create an "operating system". I really thought it was one. Then I started making a code editor. The website of it is completely vibecoded so I am scared to show that to you, lol. And I just thought that an operating system is not possible.

In October of 2024, I started learning Linux with Fortis Auris. I remember just LOVING that. In March of 2025, I installed Puppy Linux on my old computer. It broke. I kinda did not care, but later I found Linux Mint. Like, I loved that too. In fact, I actually personally love that distro (on my main PC I used it in August 2025, November 2025-February 2026) and then I tried Manjaro.

After a few months of experimentation, I decided to install EndeavourOS on my main computer on June 4th 2025. I remember disabling Secure Boot with a friend of mine. In fact, I played Ace Combat 7 like 1 hour before deleting Windows. One day later, I went on a coding conference, and told everyone I use Linux.

In August of 2025 something interesting finally happened. I realized I could make a Linux distribution, since I wanted to make an OS before! At first, I wanted to use LFS but later I decided to use an Arch base that, of course, did not make it to a release. In this stage, I thought I knew a lot about Linux. Nope. I did not know even a little bit of how the system boots or anything. At this stage, I decided to make a package manager in Python. This version is no longer used.

In December 2025 I found this really cool video of some guy making a Linux distro in like 30 minutes. I thought that was very epic, so I tried to do it. Sadly, it did not work. I was actually on vacation back then, right after Christmas. I kept working on it in January.

On January 10th 2026, I got a system up and running. And on January 12th, I managed to make a simple frontend for the installer. When I was finished with the frontend, I struggled a lot with making the actual backend. It took a long time, but in February, I managed to get it fully working.

The rest is just boring yapping about new features so I will stop here.
